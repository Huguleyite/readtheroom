# Read the Room — SEO Keyword Strategy
*Last updated: June 2026*

---

## Competitive Landscape

The "unbiased political news" space is dominated by:
- **Tangle News** — 500k readers, daily cadence, ranks for most broad "both sides" terms
- **AllSides** — huge domain authority, owns "balanced news" territory
- **Ground News** — strong on comparative media framing

**RTR's opening:** These players are daily, algorithm-assisted, and national. RTR is weekly, human-voiced, and single-topic. That specificity is a keyword asset — it opens long-tail lanes they don't compete in.

**The Frequency's opening:** The Alabama/local politics newsletter space is nearly empty. Alabama Political Reporter and Yellowhammer News are outlets, not newsletter brands. The Frequency has a real shot at owning "Alabama politics newsletter" entirely.

## Priority Order

1. **Cluster 3 (Alabama/Frequency)** — lowest competition, fastest wins
2. **Cluster 1 (Brand/Identity)** — anchor the homepage and About page
3. **Cluster 4 (Editorial articles)** — compound returns over 3–6 months
4. **Cluster 2 (RTR content)** — longer runway; builds as domain authority grows

---

## Usage Notes

- Don't stuff keywords — weave them naturally into headings, intro paragraphs, and meta descriptions
- Each issue page should have its own unique meta description using the specific topic as a keyword hook
- The game titles (Gerrymander, Pundit, Approval Machine) are themselves low-competition keywords — make sure each game page has a meta description that explains what it is
